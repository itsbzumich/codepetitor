Dear Parents and Instructor

Thank you for trusting and using my product. I’m extremely grateful for your business. I would like to take this time to tell my story. In the process, I will explain why I think it’s so important that your kids start learning competitive programming as soon as possible.

Like many of you guys, my parents cared a lot about my math abilities, giving me tons of extra problems to do outside of regular school hours. I was always pretty good at math, yet, I always knew that there would be a time when I would have to expand into other fields, such as coding. For me, my start in coding was competitive coding, as it was the easiest way to transition for me. However, it wasn’t easy. Making the switch at 15 years old, I struggled with the resources provided. A lot of the resources weren’t tailored for people like me, who didn’t know how to code and were extremely new to the field of CS. Yet, I persevered and eventually had a moderately successful competitive coding experience.

Today, I recognize the benefits of competitive coding. As a computer science major at the University of Michigan, my background in competitive coding is much more than just helping with college applications. Let's look at some other benefits. Before we do this, I'm not going to understate how useful this is for college applications. It's an extremely impressive extracurricular that can greatly boost one's chances of admission.

Success in Courses

Having a background in competitive coding helps one understand computer science concepts and makes course content a lot more learnable and interesting. With such a rigorous curriculum with little margin of error, such a benefit makes a huge difference. Your kid will be able to master the content, earn amazing grades, and feel confident leading into more challenging courses. Furthermore, the critical thinking, problem solving, and coding skills they will gain throughout competitive coding will expand beyond just computer science courses into any math, engineering, and even non-STEM classes.

Resume/Opportunities

Participating in competitive coding brings in a lot of opportunities for them to build their resume. Adding one's accomplishments within this field will allow them to stand out from the rest of the crowd. Furthermore, they will have more confidence to take advantage of other opportunities such as clubs, research, and projects. Success in this area of CS will allow for success in so many other endeavours within the field.

 

Interviews

Now the job offers will start coming in left and right! But first, you must pass a coding interview! Thankfully, there's a large intersection between coding interviews and competitive coding. You will ace every interview easily if you develop a strong foundation in competitive coding. This applies to almost any software engineering position, but also to positions in academia and more!

 

Conclusion

That’s why I made CODEPETITOR. I believe that competitive coding shouldn’t be intimidating. It should be something that should be introduced to talented youth as soon as possible, especially if they’re interested in STEM and TECH. One of my biggest regrets was not learning to code early enough. I felt like if I started during middle school, I would not only have more success in the field of competitive coding but also have been able to expand my interest in computer science beyond just problem-solving. I hope if you have the same goals for your kids, you won’t make my mistake and get them started as soon as possible. After completing this intro-level course, make sure your student continues to preserve. Continue to learn how to code, continue to problem solve, and continue to compete. I will provide resources at the end that will help guide you and your kid through the next steps. Please look for this link at the bottom of the home page.

That being said I hope you enjoy!
